    College Program: MIT MITES

    "Minority Introduction to Engineering and Science (MITES) is a rigorous six-week residential academic enrichment program for rising high school seniors – many of whom come from underrepresented or underserved communities – who have a strong academic record and are interested in studying and exploring careers in science and engineering."

    •Cost: Free! MIT covers all costs, besides transportation to and from MIT."

    •Application Begins:Fall Semester of Junior Year

    •Application Results: April of Junior Year

    Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program (SIP)

    This national program "is a free​ ​7-week introductory computer science ​program ​for​ ​10th-11th grade girls going into their junior or senior year of high school. During the 7-week program, participants​ ​learn​ about ​computer science,​ ​gain​ ​exposure​ ​to​ ​tech​ ​jobs,​ ​and​ ​join​ ​a​ sisterhood​ ​of​ ​girls​ ​who​ ​are​ ​using​ ​computer​ ​science​ ​to​ ​become​ ​change-makers.""

    •Your program depends on the company you are placed in. Companies include Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Facebook, Twitter and more.

    •Fun Fact: This website was created for the GWC Goldman Sach's SIP final project.

    •Application Process: Two Phrases (Early and Regular Deadline)

    •Cost: Free and you can be eligible to recieve up to a $1,200 stipend

    •Early Deadline: Typically opens mid-January to mid-February and recieve results in mid-March

    •Regular Deadline: Typically opens mid-February to mid-March and recieve results in mid-April

    College Program: Yale Young Global Scholars

    "Yale Young Global Scholars (YYGS) is a summer academic enrichment and leadership program for outstanding high school students from around the world. Each summer, students from over 100 countries participate in one of nine interdisciplinary sessions and immerse themselves in a global learning community."

    •Application Process: Winter till Early February

    •Application Results: Early April

    •Duration: 3 weeks long

    •Cost:$6,250 but there is financial aid

    Questbridge College Prep Scholars offers a scholarship for various summer programs, including YYGS.
